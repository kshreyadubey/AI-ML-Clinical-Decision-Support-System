# AYURA Report Rules

## Purpose

This document defines the standards and rules for generating reports within AYURA.

Reports summarize patient information, treatment progress, operational activities, and clinical documentation to support healthcare professionals in making informed decisions.

---

# Core Principle

Every report should present accurate, complete, and meaningful information.

Reports summarize information.

They never replace clinical judgment.

---

# Report Objectives

AYURA generates reports to:

- Summarize patient progress.
- Support clinical decision-making.
- Improve multidisciplinary communication.
- Track treatment effectiveness.
- Monitor hospital operations.
- Preserve historical records.

---

# Report Types

AYURA supports:

- Patient Summary Report
- Daily Clinical Report
- Progress Report
- Treatment Report
- Appointment Report
- Therapy Report
- Diet Report
- Department Report
- Discharge Summary
- AI Recommendation Report
- Audit Report

Additional report types may be added as the system evolves.

---

# Report Structure

Every report should contain:

- Report Title
- Report ID
- Generated Date
- Generated Time
- Generated By
- Patient (if applicable)
- Department (if applicable)
- Report Period
- Report Content

---

# Report Generation Rules

AYURA should ensure:

- Reports use the latest available data.
- Information is complete.
- Duplicate data is avoided.
- Missing information is clearly identified.
- Reports remain easy to understand.

---

# Patient Reports

Patient reports may include:

- Patient information
- Medical history
- Treatment plan
- Therapy progress
- Diet progress
- Appointment history
- Clinical observations
- Current status

---

# Clinical Reports

Clinical reports may include:

- Doctor observations
- Therapy outcomes
- Diet compliance
- Progress trends
- Recommendations
- Follow-up requirements

---

# Operational Reports

Operational reports may include:

- Appointment statistics
- Therapy completion
- Schedule utilization
- Workflow completion
- Documentation status

---

# AI Recommendation Reports

AI-generated reports should include:

- Recommendation
- Supporting observations
- Reasoning summary
- Confidence level (if available)

Recommendations are advisory only.

---

# Report Validation

Before generating a report, AYURA should verify:

- Required data exists.
- Information is current.
- Patient identity is correct.
- Report type is valid.
- User has permission.

---

# Confidentiality

Reports should only be accessible to authorized users.

Sensitive patient information must never be disclosed without proper authorization.

---

# Report Integrity

Reports should always be:

- Accurate
- Complete
- Consistent
- Traceable
- Secure
- Professional

---

# Archival

Generated reports should:

- Remain linked to the patient record when applicable.
- Preserve historical versions.
- Be searchable.
- Be protected from unauthorized modification.

---

# Report Statement

> AYURA generates structured, accurate, and secure reports that summarize patient care, clinical activities, and operational information. Reports enhance communication, support clinical decision-making, and maintain a reliable historical record while respecting privacy and professional responsibility.
