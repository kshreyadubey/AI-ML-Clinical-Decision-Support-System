# AYURA Appointment Rules

## Purpose

This document defines the rules governing patient appointments within AYURA.

Appointment rules ensure that consultations, therapies, follow-ups, and multidisciplinary sessions are scheduled, managed, and documented consistently.

---

# Core Principle

Every patient interaction begins with a valid appointment.

Appointments provide structure, accountability, and continuity of care.

---

# Appointment Types

AYURA supports multiple appointment types:

- New Consultation
- Follow-up Consultation
- Therapy Session
- Yoga Session
- Diet Consultation
- Acupuncture Session
- Review Consultation
- Discharge Consultation

Additional appointment types may be added as the hospital expands.

---

# Appointment Lifecycle

Every appointment follows the same lifecycle.

Scheduled

↓

Confirmed

↓

In Progress

↓

Completed

↓

Documented

If necessary, an appointment may instead become:

- Cancelled
- Rescheduled
- No Show

---

# Appointment Creation Rules

Every appointment should include:

- Patient
- Healthcare Professional
- Department
- Appointment Type
- Date
- Time
- Duration
- Status

Appointments cannot be created with incomplete mandatory information.

---

# Scheduling Rules

AYURA should ensure:

- No overlapping appointments.
- No double-booking of healthcare professionals.
- No duplicate appointments for the same purpose.
- Sufficient time for the scheduled activity.
- Proper sequencing of multidisciplinary appointments.

---

# Confirmation Rules

Appointments should be confirmed before the scheduled time.

Confirmation verifies:

- Patient availability.
- Healthcare professional availability.
- Resource availability.

---

# Rescheduling Rules

Appointments may be rescheduled when:

- Requested by the patient.
- Requested by the healthcare professional.
- Resource conflicts occur.
- Operational requirements change.

AYURA should preserve the appointment history after rescheduling.

---

# Cancellation Rules

Appointments may be cancelled only through an authorized action.

Cancellation should record:

- Reason
- User
- Timestamp

Cancelled appointments remain part of the patient's history.

---

# No Show Rules

If a patient does not attend:

- Mark the appointment as No Show.
- Record the occurrence.
- Notify the responsible healthcare professional.
- Include it in patient history.

---

# Completion Rules

An appointment is considered complete only after:

- The session has concluded.
- Required notes are recorded.
- Status is updated.

---

# Documentation Rules

Every completed appointment should generate documentation, including:

- Consultation Notes
- Therapy Notes
- Observations
- Progress Updates

Documentation should be linked to the appointment.

---

# Follow-up Rules

When a follow-up is required:

- AYURA should recommend scheduling the next appointment.
- Maintain continuity with previous records.
- Preserve the complete appointment timeline.

---

# Appointment Integrity

AYURA should ensure that appointments are:

- Accurate
- Complete
- Traceable
- Secure
- Auditable

---

# Appointment Statement

> AYURA manages appointments as the foundation of coordinated patient care. By ensuring accurate scheduling, proper documentation, and complete traceability, AYURA supports efficient multidisciplinary healthcare while maintaining continuity throughout the patient journey.
